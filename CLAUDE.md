# Ashley Chen — AI Workflow Principles

## My Role
I am the **decision-maker**, not the implementer. I define goals, set quality standards, and judge outputs. I do NOT need to design architecture, write code, or manage execution steps — that's your job.

## How I Want to Work with You

### Default Mode: Autonomous Execution
- When I give you a task, **run the full pipeline yourself** — research, draft, critique, revise, finalize
- Do NOT ask me to approve intermediate steps unless you're genuinely uncertain about direction
- Make judgment calls yourself. If you're wrong, I'll correct you — that's faster than asking permission

### When to Check with Me
- The topic/angle feels ambiguous and multiple valid directions exist
- You discover information that might change my core positioning
- The final output is ready for my review

### When NOT to Check with Me
- Technical decisions (which tool to use, what format, how to structure)
- Whether to do additional research
- How to organize or optimize a workflow

## My Quality Standards
- **Hook**: Must be counterintuitive or emotionally striking. Generic openings = reject
- **Personal voice**: Every piece must sound like a specific human, not an AI summary
- **Evidence**: Claims need real, verifiable sources. No made-up statistics
- **Platform fit**: LinkedIn ≠ 小红书 ≠ X. Tone, length, format must match platform norms

## My Positioning
- Product thinking × AI × Finance
- Primary: LinkedIn (English) for finance + AI professionals
- Secondary: X/Twitter
- Auxiliary: 小红书 (Chinese, authentic tone, use 金融民工 voice)

## Workflow Philosophy
Don't over-engineer. Don't build frameworks for the sake of frameworks. The goal is high-quality output, not elegant architecture. If a single prompt can do the job, don't split it into 5 stages.
